What makes it different
Real engineering. Real physics.
⚛️

Position-Based Dynamics

Verlet integration with iterative constraint relaxation — the same method used in game physics engines. Beams genuinely sag, bend, and fail under load.

⟷

Tension vs Compression

Every beam is tracked for its structural mode — blue = tension, orange = compression. Toggle the Force Diagram to see real load paths through your structure.

🌊

Resonance Model

Multiple vehicles cause oscillation that builds over time. Push it far enough and you'll witness a Tacoma Narrows–style resonance collapse.

📄

Engineering Reports

One click generates a printable technical report with material breakdown, stress analysis, cost efficiency, and structural notes — ready to submit.

📊

CSV Stress Export

Export per-beam stress data as a spreadsheet after every test. Analyze results in Excel or Python for deeper insights.

🎓

Guided Lessons

Four structured engineering lessons walk you through core concepts — from why beams sag, to truss geometry, to material selection.

How it works
Build. Test. Analyze.
1

Design

Drag beams between anchor points. Use screws to add junction points anywhere along a beam.

2

Test

Hit Test Bridge, then spawn vehicles. Watch stress colors build from neutral → amber → red → snap.

3

Analyze

Click any beam to inspect it. Use Force Diagram to see tension/compression. Generate a full report.

4

Improve

Replay the failure, identify weak points, rebuild. Compare two designs side by side.
